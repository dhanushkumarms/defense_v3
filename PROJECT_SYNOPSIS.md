# Project Synopsis: Enhancing LLM Robustness Against Adversarial Attacks

This research project undertakes a systematic evaluation of the safety and robustness of modern Large Language Models (LLMs), specifically focusing on Meta's `Llama-2-7B-Chat` model. While contemporary LLMs are fine-tuned to refuse harmful user requests, our investigation centers on the brittleness of these safety alignments when subjected to semantic attacks. The primary hypothesis is that these safety mechanisms are over-fitted to specific phrasings and can be bypassed by simple, non-technical methods like paraphrasing.

To test this, we conducted a controlled experiment using a curated dataset of 2,500 harmful prompts stratified across 13 content policies. Each prompt was presented to the model in three distinct variants: a `Direct` query, a `Simple Paraphrase` generated by a T5-based model, and an `Adversarial Jailbreak` prompt. The model's 7,500 responses were then evaluated for safety using a weighted ensemble of three specialized BERT-based classifiers to produce a reliable harm score.

The experimental results reveal a critical vulnerability in the model's safety architecture. While the model demonstrated strong performance against `Direct` prompts with an Attack Success Rate (ASR) of only 5.6%, its defenses were significantly compromised by semantic variations. The ASR surged to 39.0% for `Simple Paraphrase` attacks and 56.2% for `Adversarial Jailbreak` attacks. This demonstrates that the model's safety training does not generalize to understand underlying harmful intent, but rather relies on surface-level pattern matching.

In conclusion, this study provides quantitative evidence of a significant "robustness gap" in a state-of-the-art LLM. To address this, we propose future work centered on implementing and evaluating **Semantic Smoothing**, a defense mechanism designed to operate at the level of intent rather than phrasing. By verifying safety across a cluster of semantic paraphrases, this approach promises to create a more resilient and reliable safety framework, addressing the critical vulnerabilities identified in this research.
